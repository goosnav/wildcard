7.1  WHY THIS IS HARD (the 2025-2026 reality)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Apple tightened the rules on exactly this category in late 2025 / 2026 and has
  been enforcing them. Two facts shape our entire design:

  (a) Guideline 2.5.2: apps must be self-contained and may not download/execute
      code that introduces or changes the app's features. There is a narrow
      EDUCATIONAL carve-out: apps designed to teach, develop, or let users TEST
      executable code may, in limited circumstances, download code provided it
      isn't used for other purposes, AND the source must be completely viewable
      and editable by the user.

  (b) Guideline 4.7 (mini apps / HTML5): Apple permits HTML5 + JavaScript mini
      apps, but the late-2025 update REMOVED the old "web content escapes review"
      interpretation and added rules 4.7.1-4.7.5. Critically, 4.7.2 prohibits
      apps from extending or exposing native platform APIs to non-embedded
      (downloaded/generated) software WITHOUT prior Apple approval — i.e. no
      custom native bridge to generated code by default. 4.7.4 requires a
      content manifest/index of the software offered.

  Apple has acted on this: in early 2026 it removed at least one "vibe-coding"
  app from the store citing 2.5.2 and the Developer Program License section
  3.3.1(B) (interpreted code may be downloaded only if it doesn't change the
  app's primary purpose with inconsistent functionality).

  Google Play is MORE permissive about interpreted/dynamic code, but has its own
  hard requirements around AI-generated content safety (see 7.3).

  Our architecture (Doc 03) is built specifically to live inside these rules.

7.2  APPLE — THE COMPLIANT POSTURE & APPROVAL PATH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  POSITIONING (this is the strategy, not spin):
    Wild Card is presented to Apple as a PERSONAL SCRIPTING / AUTOMATION TOOL in
    which the user creates and runs their OWN tools, with the full source ALWAYS
    viewable and editable (Tenet T2; REQ-EDIT-001). This is the same lane long
    occupied by accepted scripting/coding apps. It maps directly onto the 2.5.2
    educational/developer carve-out. To the END USER the copy says "apps/tools";
    to Apple the framing is "a scripting environment with user-owned, user-
    editable source."

  WHAT WE DO TO COMPLY (design commitments):
    C1. Tools run as HTML5/JavaScript inside WebKit/JavaScriptCore (the WebView).
        No native compiled code is generated or executed. (2.5.2 / 4.7)
    C2. NO custom native API bridge is exposed to generated code on iOS.
        Device actions (share/export, file pick) are performed by the HOST's own
        native chrome, reading a result the tool handed up via WC.output
        (Doc 03 §3.6). (4.7.2)
    C3. Source of every tool is fully viewable and editable by its owner. (2.5.2)
    C4. Tools are PRIVATE to the user. We are NOT a store-within-a-store and NOT
        a marketplace/gallery of other people's tools. This avoids the "app
        store inside an app" trap and keeps the manifest scope small. (4.7)
    C5. Content manifest (4.7.4): we provide an index of the software we OFFER —
        i.e. the HOST app and the handful of TEMPLATED example tools we ship —
        plus the generation policy. A user's privately generated tools are their
        own content, disclosed via our content/safety policy and the reporting
        mechanism, not distributed by us.
    C6. AI data-sharing consent (5.1.2(i)): before the first generation we
        disclose that prompts are sent to a third-party AI provider and obtain
        consent. (REQ-PRIV-001)
    C7. Login (4.8): offer Sign in with Apple alongside any third-party/social
        login used to set up the primary account.
    C8. Egress is allow-listed (REQ-RUN-005); generated tools cannot become a
        conduit to arbitrary or disallowed content/services.

  ACCOUNT & SUBMISSION MECHANICS:
    - Enroll in the Apple Developer Program (~US$99/year). Org enrollment (D-U-N-S
      number) recommended over individual for a commercial product.
    - Build signing, App Store Connect record, App Privacy ("nutrition label")
      completed accurately (REQ-PRIV-003).
    - Subscriptions sold via StoreKit in-app purchase (REQ-PAY-002). Apple's
      commission applies; the Small Business Program reduces it to 15% for
      developers under the revenue threshold (us, initially) — enroll in it.
    - Distribute beta via TestFlight; submit for App Review.

  REVIEW EXPECTATIONS & TIMELINE:
    - Initial review of a code-generation app should be assumed to draw EXTRA
      scrutiny. Budget 2-3 review rounds. Typical review turnaround is on the
      order of a day or two per round, but plan for longer on a novel category.
    - Prepare a reviewer note explaining the scripting/automation positioning,
      the source-visible/editable design, the absence of a native bridge, the
      content-safety + reporting controls, and the egress allow-list. Make the
      reviewer's job easy.
    - Have a designated demo account and 2-3 pre-made example tools so the
      reviewer immediately sees benign, useful, self-contained behavior.

  IF APPLE STILL REJECTS:
    - Escalate via the Resolution Center with the positioning note; if needed,
      request a call / App Review Board appeal.
    - Fallback channels (7.5): ship the web/PWA version (no App Review), and/or
      lead with Android, while iterating the iOS posture.

7.3  GOOGLE PLAY — APPROVAL PATH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Google Play permits this category more readily, but enforces an AI-Generated
  Content policy and the general Developer Program Policies. Requirements:

    G1. AI-Generated Content policy: the app must PREVENT generation of
        prohibited content (CSAM and anything sexualizing minors; non-consensual
        sexual deepfakes; content enabling scams/deception; content promoting
        self-harm or dangerous acts; deceptive election content; harassment).
        Implement input + output safety screening (CMP-12) accordingly.
    G2. In-app reporting: provide a way for users to flag offensive generated
        content, and feed those reports into moderation/filters. (REQ-PRIV-002)
    G3. Comply with all other policies: Malware / Mobile Unwanted Software,
        Device & Network Abuse (relevant to any dynamic code — keep it sandboxed
        and non-deceptive), User Data + the Data Safety form, Deceptive Behavior,
        Intellectual Property, and Families policy if applicable (we age-gate
        and are NOT directed to children — REQ-PRIV-005).
    G4. AI transparency/labeling expectations are rising; disclose AI use and
        keep moderation evidence (also helps EU AI Act — 7.4).

  ACCOUNT & SUBMISSION MECHANICS:
    - Google Play Developer account (one-time ~US$25). Identity verification
      required. Organization account for a commercial product.
    - Subscriptions via Google Play Billing (REQ-PAY-002); commission with the
      reduced 15% tier for the first revenue band applies.
    - Complete the Data Safety form accurately; provide a privacy policy URL.
    - Release through internal -> closed -> open testing tracks; expect a
      pre-launch review. New personal/financial-data or AI apps may face added
      identity and policy checks; budget time for these.

  REVIEW EXPECTATIONS:
    - Review can take from hours to several days; new accounts and AI apps trend
      slower. The substantive risk is the AI-content policy, not dynamic code —
      so the moderation + reporting build (CMP-12) is the gating work for Play.

7.4  CROSS-CUTTING LEGAL / REGULATORY (verify with counsel — OQ-02)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - EU AI Act: transparency duties for AI systems that generate content
    (users informed they're interacting with AI; AI-generated content
    appropriately disclosed); broader obligations phase in over 2025-2026+.
    Affects EU availability and your disclosures.
  - United States: a patchwork of state requirements (e.g. disclosure/
    transparency and bias-related rules in several states) plus evolving federal
    direction. Maintain clear AI-use disclosure and records.
  - General: a clear Privacy Policy and Terms of Service; data-subject rights
    (access/export/delete — REQ-DATA-004); a defined data-retention policy for
    prompts and generated content; processor terms with the AI provider.
  - AI provider terms: using the model/agent SDK to power an end-user product is
    governed by the provider's COMMERCIAL terms, and you must authenticate with
    your own API key/billing (not a consumer chat login). Confirm at start.

7.5  FALLBACK & SEQUENCING STRATEGY (de-risk early)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - The Phase 0 spike (Doc 05, M0) MUST include a skeleton submission test of the
    scripting/automation posture on BOTH stores, before heavy investment.
  - WEB / PWA FIRST is a legitimate and fast lead channel: it sidesteps App
    Review entirely, reaches paying users immediately (web checkout), reuses the
    same Runtime SDK + bundle format, and proves demand (Doc 08). iOS PWAs have
    capability limits, but for the in-scope tool catalog (Doc 01 §6) that is
    largely acceptable. Strongly consider launching here first.
  - LEAD WITH ANDROID for the native experience if Apple posture is uncertain;
    add iOS once the positioning is validated.
  - Keep the iOS build's capability surface minimal by design (Doc 03 §3.9) so
    there is the least possible for a reviewer to object to.

  [ ] Account + data export and delete available in-app.                     (REQ-DATA-004)

  [ ] IAP via StoreKit / Play Billing; Small Business / reduced-rate enrolled.
  [ ] Live guidelines re-checked within 7 days of submission.                    (OQ-05)
